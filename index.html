Digital Card Payments, Explained

From online checkout to settlement – how digital card payments actually move.

Compare Digital-First Cards

What is “digital pay” in card terms?

DigiPay covers all the ways a card can be used without plastic: online checkouts, in-app purchases, recurring billing, wallets and tokenized card-on-file.

  • • Online one-time payments
  • • In-app and in-game purchases
  • • Subscriptions & recurring billing
  • • Stored cards at merchants (card-on-file)
  • • Mobile & web wallets

Typical digital payment flow

1. Checkout

Customer enters card data, uses a wallet button or a stored card.

2. Gateway

Payment gateway formats the transaction and sends it to the processor.

3. Processor & network

Processor connects to Visa/Mastercard and the issuing bank.

4. Authorization

Issuer checks funds, risk and 3-D Secure/Strong Customer Authentication.

5. Capture & settlement

Funds are captured and later settled into the merchant account.

Risk, SCA and 3-D Secure

Digital card payments rely heavily on risk engines and step-up authentication:

  • • Device and browser fingerprinting
  • • Behavioural data (location, velocity, history)
  • • 3-D Secure / Strong Customer Authentication prompts
  • • Exemptions for low-risk, low-value or recurring payments

Digital-first card products

A “digital-first” card is designed for online and wallet usage as the primary channel:

  • • Instant virtual card after approval
  • • Strong wallet integration (Apple / Google Pay)
  • • Rewards on online or wallet transactions
  • • Easy number replacement from the app
  • • Controls for subscriptions and merchant categories
