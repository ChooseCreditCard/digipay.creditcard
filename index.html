Digital Card Payments, Explained

From online checkout to settlement – how digital card payments actually move.

Compare Digital-First Cards

What is “digital pay” in card terms?

DigiPay covers all the ways a card can be used without plastic: online checkouts, in-app purchases, recurring billing, wallets and tokenized card-on-file.

  • Online one-time payments
  • In-app and in-game purchases
  • Subscriptions & recurring billing
  • Stored cards at merchants (card-on-file)
  • Mobile & web wallets

Typical digital payment flow

1. Checkout

Card entry, wallet button or saved card.

2. Gateway

Gateway formats & forwards transaction.

3. Processor & network

Visa/Mastercard rails + issuing bank.

4. Authorization

Risk checks, SCA, issuer decision.

5. Settlement

Funds captured and settled to merchant.

Risk, SCA and 3-D Secure

Digital payments rely heavily on risk intelligence and step-up authentication:

  • Device and browser fingerprinting
  • Behavioural data (location, velocity, patterns)
  • 3-D Secure / Strong Customer Authentication
  • Exemptions for low-risk or recurring billing

Digital-first card products

These cards are designed for wallets & online-first usage:

  • Instant virtual card after approval
  • Strong Apple/Google Pay integration
  • Rewards on online and wallet transactions
  • Easy number replacement
  • Controls for subscriptions & merchant types
